# Boston Red Sox

**Purpose:** Track the Boston Red Sox as a daily baseball briefing topic, combining structured game/standings context with official, beat-reporter, and reputable news signals.

---

## Trusted Sources

1. **Official team and league sources**
   - Red Sox and MLB official channels are the starting point for confirmed roster moves, lineups, injuries, transactions, and schedule information.
   - Caveat: official channels may lag beat reporters on breaking context and usually avoid hard evaluation.

2. **Boston and MLB beat reporting**
   - Use reputable beat reporters and established outlets for injury detail, lineup explanations, clubhouse quotes, prospect movement, and roster implications.
   - Beat-reporter signal should be prioritized over generic fan or aggregator commentary.

3. **Structured sports data**
   - Use MLB schedule, linescore, probable pitcher, and standings data for game results, next-game context, and AL East positioning.
   - Treat structured results as factual context; use reporting to explain why the result mattered.

---

## Core Framework

### The fundamental question

What materially changed in the Red Sox picture since the last briefing, and does it affect the next game, roster outlook, division position, or season trajectory?

### Scorecard or map

- Recent games: final score, opponent, winning/losing pitcher when available, save when relevant, and concise game note.
- Next game: opponent, venue, time, probable starters when available.
- Standings: AL East rank, record, games back, streak, and recent form when available.
- Roster health: IL moves, day-to-day injuries, rehab assignments, pitching availability, and call-ups/options.
- Prospect/depth movement: only include when it affects the major league club or a high-profile prospect.

### Key drivers

- Starting pitching health and workload.
- Bullpen availability and recent usage.
- Lineup health, especially core hitters and defensive alignment.
- AL East and wild-card positioning.
- Credible trade, transaction, and player-development signals.

## Open Threads / Things to Watch

- Whether recent results change the AL East or wild-card picture.
- Injuries that affect the next series or roster construction.
- Pitching depth, rotation changes, and bullpen workload.
- Lineup changes driven by performance, matchups, or health.
- Prospect call-ups, options, rehab assignments, and trade rumors from credible sources.

---

## Format Notes for Daily Briefings

- Lead with what changed since the last briefing.
- Include structured results/standings/next-game context when sports data is available.
- Do not overreact to a single game unless it changes standings, injuries, roster posture, or a trend.
- Separate confirmed reporting from fan chatter and rumor.
- Link reported news and quotes; structured score/standings rows do not need social citations.
