# Topic Wizard Answers

Answer these before the topic is allowed to run in the briefing.

1. **What decision should this topic help you make?**
   - Understand the broad U.S. equity-market backdrop through the S&P 500 / SPY: price action, major macro catalysts, rates/inflation context, earnings tone, and whether the move has a clear sourced explanation.

2. **What is the ideal briefing length: 3 bullets, 1 paragraph, or deeper analysis?**
   - Concise market desk summary: price action first, then 2-4 catalyst bullets and watch next.

3. **Which sources do you trust most?**
   - Structured quote data for SPY, major financial wires and outlets, Federal Reserve/BLS/BEA/Treasury releases, company earnings calendars, and reputable market strategists when clearly sourced.

4. **Which sources are useful but need caution?**
   - Market blogs, live blogs, analyst-commentary roundups, finance-media explainers, and social posts. Use them for context only when they point to a concrete catalyst.

5. **Which sources should be excluded?**
   - Stock-pick spam, signal groups, Discord/Telegram promotions, unsourced crash/rally claims, crypto-only narratives, single-name hype unless it affects the index, and generic technical-analysis posts.

6. **What keywords, accounts, tickers, sports teams, geographies, or companies define the topic?**
   - SPY, S&P 500, SPX, U.S. stocks, equities, Federal Reserve, Fed, Treasury yields, inflation, CPI, PCE, jobs report, unemployment, earnings, guidance, mega-cap tech, breadth, VIX, and risk appetite.

7. **What should count as high priority?**
   - Large SPY/S&P 500 moves, Fed/rate developments, inflation or labor-market data, Treasury-yield shocks, broad earnings-guide changes, index breadth deterioration/improvement, volatility spikes, and major geopolitical or policy events affecting equities.

8. **What should be ignored as noise?**
   - Price movement alone without a sourced catalyst, one-off pundit predictions, intraday squiggles, chart-only posts, single-name stories with no index implication, and recycled market-wrap articles.

9. **Should the section include links to source posts/articles every time?**
   - Use links for news catalysts and quoted analysis. The SPY price-action block can come from structured market data without social links.

10. **Should the tone be neutral analyst, market-oriented, fan of a team, or executive summary?**
    - Market-oriented executive summary: calm, catalyst-focused, and explicit when a move has no clear sourced explanation.

11. **Which X accounts are reliable enough to prioritize for this topic?**
    - None by default for the public template. This topic should rely mostly on structured market data and news context because broad market X search is too noisy.
