# S&P 500 Market

**Purpose:** Provide a broad U.S. equity-market snapshot centered on SPY as the S&P 500 ticker proxy, with structured price action and a short catalyst readout.

---

## Trusted Sources

1. **Structured market data**
   - SPY quote and recent daily movement are the anchor for the section.
   - Price action is context, not a catalyst by itself.

2. **Primary macro and policy sources**
   - Federal Reserve, BLS, BEA, Treasury, and other official releases matter when they explain rates, inflation, growth, labor, or liquidity changes.

3. **Reputable market reporting**
   - Major financial wires and established outlets can identify likely catalysts, earnings themes, policy developments, and sector leadership.
   - Caveat: market-wrap stories often reverse-engineer explanations after price moves. Label uncertainty.

---

## Core Framework

### The fundamental question

What moved the S&P 500 / SPY, if anything, and is the move backed by a concrete catalyst or just ordinary market noise?

### Scorecard or map

- SPY latest/intraday move and prior completed daily move.
- Major macro catalyst: Fed, rates, inflation, labor, growth, Treasury yields, or policy.
- Earnings catalyst: index-level earnings tone, mega-cap leadership, or guidance revisions.
- Risk tone: volatility, breadth, credit/rates pressure, geopolitical or policy shock.
- Signal quality: clearly sourced catalyst, plausible but unconfirmed catalyst, or no obvious catalyst.

### Key drivers

- Rate expectations and Treasury yields.
- Inflation and labor-market data.
- Earnings revisions, margins, and mega-cap leadership.
- Market breadth and volatility.
- Policy/geopolitical shocks with broad equity impact.

## Open Threads / Things to Watch

- Whether index moves are broad-based or concentrated in a few mega-cap names.
- Whether rate moves confirm or contradict equity-market action.
- Whether macro data changes Fed-cut/hike expectations.
- Whether earnings guidance is improving or deteriorating at the index level.
- Whether volatility or breadth signals a change in risk appetite.

---

## Format Notes for Daily Briefings

- Start with SPY price action and state whether a sourced catalyst is visible.
- Keep the section short unless there is a major macro, Fed, earnings, or volatility event.
- Avoid social-market noise by default.
- Do not treat analyst predictions, technical chart posts, or single-name hype as broad-market signal unless they clearly affect the index.
- Distinguish "what happened" from "why people say it happened."
