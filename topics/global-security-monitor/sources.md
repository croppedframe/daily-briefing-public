# Global Security Monitor Sources

This public example intentionally uses source categories instead of a personal source hierarchy.

## Prefer

- Official public statements from relevant institutions.
- Reputable wire services and established reporting organizations.
- Local reporting with named reporters, clear sourcing, and updates/corrections.
- Specialist public-source analysts who cite evidence, methods, and confidence levels.
- Public advisories, humanitarian updates, infrastructure notices, and institutional reports.

## Use With Caution

- Fast-moving social posts before corroboration.
- Aggregator accounts that summarize without primary links.
- Translated snippets without a clear original source.
- Single-source claims about casualties, control, intent, or escalation.
- Satellite, geolocation, or logistics analysis without methods or caveats.

## Exclude

- Private accounts, leaked material, or paywalled scraping.
- Graphic content unless essential to a material public development.
- Recycled footage or old images presented as new.
- Anonymous rumor threads.
- Engagement bait, panic framing, and posts that encourage harassment.

## Handling Rules

- Prefer official/public reporting and corroborated specialist reporting.
- Link material claims to sources.
- Label unconfirmed, disputed, or single-source claims clearly.
- Do not sensationalize.
- Do not infer intent or escalation from a single post.
