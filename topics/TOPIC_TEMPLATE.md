## Trusted Sources

List public sources or source categories in priority order. Put the most reliable daily starting points first.

1. **Official or primary source**
   - Why it matters.
   - How often it updates.
   - Caveats and blind spots.

2. **Specialist reporter, analyst, tracker, or domain expert**
   - Why it matters.
   - Whether it gives breaking news, interpretation, or both.
   - What should be corroborated before use.

3. **General public search**
   - Search targets: official statements, company releases, filings, local reporting, specialist reporting, market data, sports data, or other public records.
   - Exclusions: paywalled scraping, private accounts, leaked material, or claims that cannot be sourced to public material.

---

## Source Confidence Rules

- Label unconfirmed, disputed, or single-source claims clearly.
- Prefer official/public reporting and corroborated specialist reporting.
- Do not treat engagement, virality, or repetition as confirmation.
- Do not sensationalize developing events.
- Avoid graphic detail unless it is essential to understanding the development.

## Core Framework

Stable context the model should remember and update against, not re-explain every day.

### The fundamental question

State the durable question that new information should answer.

### Scorecard or map

- Current status by topic, geography, company, team, market, organization, or conflict.
- Known pending decisions or events.
- What would change the status.
- For stock topics with `marketData.symbol`, include prior completed daily move and latest/intraday move in every briefing.

### Key drivers

- Driver one.
- Driver two.
- Driver three.

## Format Notes for Daily Briefings

- Lead with what changed since the last briefing.
- Connect new items back to the core framework.
- Do not restate background unless it changed.
- Label uncertainty clearly.
- Separate confirmed developments from claims that still need corroboration.
- For stock topics, include a distinct price-action item when market data is available and state whether the move has a sourced catalyst or is context only.
- Stay concise and analytical.
